Direct Mail Marketing Program

Here’s what’s great about every door direct.Instead of mailing to specific names and addresses, the United States Postal Services Every Door Direct Mail Program (EDDM) program allows you to have your mail pieces delivered to individual neighborhoods(by carrier routes). You simply select the carrier route on the USPS “Every Door Direct Online Tool” and pinpoint your audience that you would like to deliver to. The result will be that the USPS will deliver directly to every mailbox on that route.

With Every Door Direct mail service there’s no need to buy addresses or mailing lists. Instead, you can focus your real estate agent marketing on potential customers within a specific radius of the zip code or area you want to target. This is extremely beneficial to real estate agents since this is the same strategy that agents use when we choose a farming area to market in. So, if you currently have an area that you are farming you can blanket mail to the same area without having to every home address and without paying the cost of standard first class mail. The cost savings of the Every Door Direct Marketing are huge and you can save up to 30% to 70% off of the normal mailing cost by using this extremely effective mailing program.

Main Benefits of EDDM Mail Program:

  • No Mailing List Needed → Saturate an entire area or zip code (PERFECT for agent farms)
  • No Postage Permit Needed → Some large mailings need a permit that can cost up to $450 (With EDDM program your permit fee is waived)
  • Stand out from the Competition by Mailing “Larger” Pieces → Sthe USPS charges one price regardless of the size of your marketing piece.
  • Current cost per piece → at the USPS is 17.7 cents compared to 49 cents for a first class stamp.

THE PROBLEM WITH THE WAY AGENTS CURRENTLY USE DIRECT MAIL

95% of real estate agents direct mail marketing campaigns are designed to target listings (sellers). Most agents fail with direct mail and end up quitting this highly effective source of lead generation before they have ever really given it a chance. This is really unfortunate because direct mail marketing when done the right way is extremely effective and can yield massive results.

Here are the two major reasons why agents fail with direct mail:

  1. Their direct mail marketing strategy is flawed. In most cases agents sent out their postcards hoping to time the arrival of a postcard with the exact time the home seller is ready to sell. The likelihood of a homeowner “holding on to a postcard” until they are ready to sell is slim to none.
  2. When agents send out there “just sold” or “just listed” direct mail campaigns, there is no clear Call To Action or the Call To Action is asking the seller to do too much. We have found in most cases that the agent’s call to action requests that the seller “call now” to list their home. Now think about that for a moment. You are asking a total stranger who has never met you and only knows you from the mailings to “pick up the phone and talk to you about selling their home”. You are asking a complete stranger to make a call to you so that you can handle the most important financial transaction of their life. You’re asking them to do an “all or nothing” thing. And even if they were interested in your services at some other point in time, you have no way of capturing their contact information unless they actually call you.

So, when you “hope that your mailing reaches the seller at the exact same time they are ready to sell” and you ask that homeowner to “just call you” to list their home, what do you think the odds are that is really going to happen? LOW, LOW, LOW. The good news is that we do have the solution that will stack the odds in your favor and that will absolutely produce the results that you SHOULD OF BEEN getting long ago in your direct mail campaign.

STEP 1: DECIDE WHAT CAMPAIGN YOU WILL RUN- “Just Listed or Just Sold”

Regardless of which campaign you go with, you will be targeting homeowners (SELLERS) within a radius of the property you will be using for your “Just Listed or Just Sold” postcard. The most flexible choice would be the “Just Sold” campaign. This is because you can either use a listing that you “Just Sold” or use an office co-workers listing OR you can go on the MLS and locate another agents listing that “Just Sold” in the area that you want to mail to. However CONSULT YOUR BROKER IF YOU CHOOSE TO MARKET ANOTHER AGENTS “JUST SOLD” LISTING SINCE RULES VARY FROM MLS TO MLS.

If you choose to do a “Just Listed” campaign, this is perfect when you have your own listing to market. If you do not have your own “Just Listed” listing, YOU WILL ABSOLUTELY NEED PERMISSION from the listing agent to advertise their property. However, we have found that in 90% of the cases MOST agents will give you permission because they are happy to get the help with the caveat that they need to see your marketing piece first.

Once you have decided on the campaign you will run, you will need a clear “Front Photo” of the home you are going to feature. This will be used in your postcard so be sure it’s a photo that you think will work well.

REMEMBER: Regardless of which campaign you run, you will be targeting Homeowners who are looking to sell that are located within a radius of the property you choose
